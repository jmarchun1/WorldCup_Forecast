📐 Methodology & Calculations
How forecasts, scoring, and Brier are computed
This dashboard runs 12 large language models against every World Cup 2026 group stage match. Each model receives a structured prompt including historical base rates, team injury data, style ratings, real-time match context, and prediction market implied probabilities. Models output a predicted scoreline, three outcome probabilities (home win / draw / away win), and a confidence score.

BRIER SCORE (probability calibration)
Brier = (phome − Ihome)² + (pdraw − Idraw)² + (paway − Iaway)²
Where p is the model's predicted probability and I is 1 if that outcome occurred, 0 otherwise. This is the undivided multi-class Brier score (range 0–2.0), matching the convention used in football forecasting literature (e.g. onthepitch.now).

Worked example: Model predicts home win 50%, draw 30%, away win 20%. Actual: draw.
Brier = (0.50−0)² + (0.30−1)² + (0.20−0)² = 0.25 + 0.49 + 0.04 = 0.780
Benchmark: onthepitch.now (Elo + Dixon-Coles + Poisson, no market prior) scores 0.572 on this tournament. Our ensemble (with prediction market odds in prompt) scores approximately 0.494.
ENSEMBLE & BAYESIAN WEIGHTING
The consensus prediction is a Bayesian-weighted average across all 12 models. Model weights are updated after each scored match based on cumulative Brier score — models with better calibration receive higher weight in subsequent ensembles. Weights are stored in model_weights.json and rebuilt after each scoring run. Scoreline votes (e.g. "8/12 models predict 1-1") use equal weights.
EXPECTED VALUE (EV)
EV measures whether a model's probability implies edge over the prediction market price.
EV = p_model × (decimal_odds − 1) − (1 − p_model)
Positive EV means the model believes the outcome is more likely than the market implies. A value bet is flagged when EV > 0.05 on any outcome. Market odds are vig-removed implied probabilities sourced from prediction markets.

A/B RESEARCH CONDITION
A parallel set of forecasts (forecasts_research/) are run on the same matches with prediction market odds removed from the prompt. Comparing Brier scores between Condition A (with odds) and Condition B (no odds) measures how much of LLM forecasting accuracy comes from the market prior vs genuine LLM reasoning — the central question of the accompanying research paper.
